Introduction

What is Design Thinking?

Design Thinking is a human-centered approach to innovation that emphasizes understanding user needs and creating solutions that truly work. It's a process for creative problem-solving that starts with the people you're designing for.

Project Overview

This week, we applied the Design Thinking methodology to create a personalized LED nameplate for an office desk. The project challenged us to understand user needs, define problems clearly, generate creative solutions, and visualize our concepts through annotated sketches.

The 5-Stage Design Thinking Process

1. Empathize

Understand your users deeply through observation and engagement

2. Define

Frame the problem based on user needs and insights

3. Ideate

Generate a wide range of creative solutions

4. Prototype

Build testable representations of your ideas

5. Test

Learn from users and refine your solution

Note: This week we focused on the first four stages of the Design Thinking process.

Process Documentation

Phase 1: Empathize & Define

Understanding User Needs

We began by creating an empathy map to understand office workers' experiences with desk identification:

SAY

  • "I want something that represents my personality"
  • "It should be visible but not distracting"
  • "I'd like something modern and professional"

DO

  • Use temporary paper name tags
  • Place business cards on desk
  • Have no name identification at all

THINK

  • "My workspace should reflect my identity"
  • "First impressions matter when colleagues visit"
  • "I want something functional but also aesthetically pleasing"

FEEL

  • Frustrated with generic office supplies
  • Want to express individuality within professional constraints
  • Excited about personalizing workspace

Problem Statement

Office professionals need a way to display their name and role in a personalized, professional manner because current identification methods are generic and don't allow for individual expression while maintaining workplace professionalism.

Phase 2: Ideate & Prototype

Ideation Process

Following the ideation rules of quantity over quality and deferring judgment, we generated 10+ creative solutions including:

  • Minimalist acrylic plate with edge-lit LED name
  • Wooden nameplate with recessed LED letters
  • Magnetic modular nameplate with interchangeable role titles
  • 3D printed nameplate with integrated LED lighting
  • Eco-friendly bamboo nameplate with solar-powered LED

Concept Sketches & Annotations

We selected the three most promising concepts and created annotated sketches to visualize each solution:

Concept 1: Minimalist Acrylic LED Nameplate

A sleek acrylic nameplate with edge-lit LED illumination. Features laser-etched text and a subtle 15° angled stand.

  • Materials: Clear acrylic, white LED strips
  • Power: USB-C connection
  • Dimensions: 200mm × 60mm × 5mm
  • Features: Professional glow effect

Concept 2: Modular Magnetic Nameplate

A system of interchangeable magnetic components allowing users to update their role or add information.

  • Materials: Magnetic base, interchangeable plates
  • Power: Rechargeable battery
  • Features: Flexible, updatable
  • Advantage: Adaptable to changing roles

Concept 3: Eco-Friendly Bamboo Nameplate

Sustainable bamboo nameplate with solar-powered LED illumination, combining natural materials with technology.

  • Materials: Bamboo, solar panel, LEDs
  • Power: Solar-powered with battery
  • Features: Sustainable, self-powered
  • Advantage: Environmentally friendly

Selected Concept

After evaluation based on feasibility, desirability, and viability, we selected the Minimalist Acrylic LED Nameplate as it best balances professional appearance with personalization and is feasible to manufacture with available tools.

Reflection

Learning Outcomes

This week introduced the structured approach of Design Thinking for problem-solving. The process of moving from empathy to defined problem to creative solutions provided a clear framework for tackling design challenges. The emphasis on understanding user needs before jumping to solutions was particularly valuable.

Technical Challenges

  • Creating a comprehensive Empathy Map that captures diverse user perspectives
  • Crafting a precise Problem Statement that accurately reflects user needs
  • Generating truly diverse ideas without self-censoring during the ideation phase
  • Creating clear, communicative sketches that effectively convey design concepts
  • Balancing creativity with feasibility when selecting concepts

Key Takeaways

  • Design Thinking provides a structured yet flexible approach to problem-solving
  • Empathy is the foundation of good design - understanding users is crucial
  • A well-defined problem statement guides the entire design process
  • Divergent thinking (ideation) followed by convergent thinking (selection) yields better solutions
  • Simple sketches can effectively communicate complex ideas
  • The iterative nature of Design Thinking allows for continuous improvement

Design Sprint Deliverables

  • 10+ Creative Ideas: Diverse range of potential solutions generated through ideation
