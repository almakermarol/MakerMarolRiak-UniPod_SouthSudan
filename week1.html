Introduction

Welcome to the UniPod Digital Fabrication Program - where ideas transform into reality! This isn't just another course - it's my gateway to becoming a true maker. Over the next 12 months, I'll develop the skills, mindset, and confidence to bring any concept from sketch to functional prototype.

This program emphasizes hands-on, project-based learning where I'll tackle real problems, make real mistakes, and create real solutions that work in the real world. The goal isn't perfection from day one, but progress, curiosity, and the courage to turn ideas into reality.

The Maker Mindset

As the orientation materials emphasized, prototyping is beautifully chaotic. I'm learning to celebrate curiosity, collaboration, and the creative process - understanding that failures are just learning in disguise!

Key Principles I'm Embracing:

  • Learn by Doing: Theory meets practice in hands-on projects
  • Idea to Prototype: Master the complete journey from concept to functional prototype
  • Embrace the Glorious Mess: Prototyping is beautifully chaotic - celebrate curiosity and collaboration
  • Fail Forward: Failures are just learning in disguise

Safety First

Safety is a mindset, not just a rule. Before we can create amazing things, we need to ensure we're working in a safe environment.

Safety Guidelines I'm Following:

  • Know Your Equipment: Never touch a tool I haven't been properly trained on
  • Dress the Part: No loose clothing, jewelry, or open-toe shoes in the lab
  • Protect Your Senses: Safety glasses are mandatory - no exceptions
  • Mental Safety Matters: Ask questions freely and frequently

Process Documentation

Safety Scavenger Hunt Process:

My first task was to complete a virtual safety scavenger hunt, walking through the makerspace with detective eyes to identify 3 safety hazards and 3 exemplary safety practices. This exercise trained my maker intuition to spot risks before they become problems.

Identified Safety Hazards:

  1. Extension cords creating trip hazards across walkways
  2. Unsecured materials near the edge of workbenches
  3. Inadequate ventilation in the laser cutting area

Exemplary Safety Practices:

  1. Clear safety signage and emergency stop buttons on all major equipment
  2. Properly organized and labeled personal protective equipment stations
  3. Well-maintained tool storage with clear inspection records

Reflection

What I Learned:

This first week has been an exciting introduction to the maker mindset and the UniPod program. The emphasis on safety as a mindset rather than just a set of rules resonated with me - it's about developing an intuitive awareness of potential risks and taking proactive measures.

I appreciate how the program balances technical skills with community building. Setting up GitHub and connecting with fellow makers through WhatsApp created an immediate sense of belonging to a community of creators.

Challenges and How I Overcame Them:

The safety scavenger hunt required careful observation and critical thinking about spaces I might normally take for granted. Setting up a professional GitHub profile pushed me to think about my online presence and how I want to present myself to the maker community.

Looking Ahead:

I'm excited to begin the hands-on work in the coming weeks and start building the assistive devices I'm passionate about. The connections I've made this week will be invaluable as we support each other through the learning process.

Key Takeaways:

  • Safety is an ongoing mindset, not a one-time lesson
  • Documentation begins on day one with GitHub setup
